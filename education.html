Chelmsford High

For my freshman and sophomore years of high school, I went to Chelmsford High School. Chelmsford High has about 1,500 to 2,000 students, so it was pretty packed due to the aging infrastructure. Lunch time was especially bad. I did well in my classes, earning high honor roll each semester. However, I wanted to learn more and have more opportunities, so I decided to go to Mass Academy.

Junior Year

Junior year is an intense courseload with classes that exceed AP depth. We took classes in physics, math modeling, science and technical writing , foreign language (Spanish for me), STEM I AND II, computer science, and humanities. In physics, we learn a curriculum that covers both AP Physics 1 and SAT Physics topics. The workload in physics is thusly large, with homework every night in addition to practice problems and labs. Math modeling is a course that is about one-third based in modeling real life problems in teams with no "right answer" but instead guidelines to follow. The other two-thirds or so covers material similar to precalculus and is learnt by doing with no formal lectures. Science and technical writing, or STW, covers how to write technical materials and ensuring clarity and grammatical correctness in STEM papers. Foreign language is a full immersion class, meaning that only the target language can be spoken in class. This class covered a lot of cultural aspects as well and I found it to be enriching and interesting. STEM I follows the development of an individual project. A page on that is coming soon. STEM II is a group project where each team creates an assistive technology. More on that soon as well. In computer science, all students create their own websites at the beginning of the year and an app at the end of the year. In the middle, most students learn Java, but those that already know it complete an independent project. You can see mine on the Projects and Research Page, which also includes the app I made with my team. Last but certainly not least, Humanities is a sort of dual purpose course, covering both English and History, as well as cultural aspects such as art. In Humanities, we discuss a wide plethora of literature and do many essays to improve our writing and grammatical skills. A portfolio with work from my classes in Junior year is in development and will be posted soon.


Mass Academy.

Senior Year

Senior year is a chance for students to take all their courses at WPI, essentially as a freshman in college. This year, I'm taking courses in Mechanics, Robotics, Discrete Mathematics, Algorithms, and Calculus I-IV. We are also required to take a number of Humanities courses, for which I'm taking Macroeconomics, Psychology, Science Literature, and British Literature. Another important part of senior year is the Senior Independent Study Project (SISP). You can see mine on the Projects and Research page.

While doing my SISP, I've also been learning about the joys of an awesome shell program called cowsay.
